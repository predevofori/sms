System Overview

ShiftMaster is designed to streamline your workforce management process with intuitive tools for scheduling, team building, and reporting.

Key Components

ShiftMaster - Main Components

Dashboard

Your central hub showing key metrics and quick access to all features.

Shift Management

Create and manage shifts for your workforce with date and time specifications.

Employee Management

Maintain a database of all employees with their roles and contact information.

Organize employees into functional teams for efficient shift assignments.

System Requirements

ShiftMaster Supports Windows And Mac. With an integration on DNATA'S HHD's via and Andriod App. For optimal performance, we recommend:

  • Windows And Mac
  • Minimum screen resolution of 1280x720
  • Stable internet connection for cloud features

Data Structure

The system organizes information in a hierarchical structure:

  1. Employers - The organizations or companies.
  2. Employees - Individual workers with specific roles.
  3. Shifts - Time periods for work assignments.
  4. Teams - Groups of employees assigned to specific shifts.
  5. Assignments - Individual work allocations.

Shift Management

ShiftMaster allows you to efficiently create and manage shifts for your workforce. This section covers all aspects of shift management.

Creating a New Shift

  1. Navigate to the Shift Schedule section
  2. Select the desired date using the date picker
  3. Click the Add Team button
  4. Fill in the shift details:
    • Shift type (Morning or Night)
    • Location (e.g., FG5, FG4)
    • Area (e.g., Breakdown, Delivery)
    • Team members (Staff, Porters, Forklift Operator)
    • Optional supervisor
  5. Click Save Team to create the shift
Create a Shift

Viewing and Editing Shifts

To view or edit existing shifts:

  1. Navigate to the Shift Schedule section
  2. Select the date you want to view
  3. Click on a shift to view details
  4. Use the Edit button to make changes
  5. Click Save to update the shift
Shift Types

ShiftMaster supports two main shift types:

  • Morning Shift: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM
  • Night Shift: 6:00 PM to 6:00 AM

These can be customized in the Settings section if your organization uses different shift times.

Best Practices for Shift Management Alongside HHD's

  • Break Schedule Notifications. When break starts and when break ends.
  • Updates Staffs On Team Members Per Shift.
  • A common chat Forum to collaborate with other employees
  • A private chat option to connect and report issues and damages to Supervisors
  • Announcement/alert Pop up notifications

Employee Management

The Employee Management section allows you to maintain a complete database of all your workforce members, their roles, and contact information.

Adding a New Employee

  1. Navigate to the Employees section
  2. Click the Add Employee button
  3. Fill in the employee details:
    • First and last name
    • Employee ID (optional)
    • Employer (select from dropdown)
    • Role (Staff, Porter, Forklift Operator, or Supervisor)

Employee Roles Explained

Role Definitions
  • Staff Member: General workforce with various responsibilities
  • Porter: Handles luggage and cargo movement (typically 2 per team)
  • Forklift Operator: Certified to operate forklift equipment (typically 1 per team)
  • Supervisor: Team leader with additional responsibilities (optional per team)

Searching and Filtering Employees

You can easily find specific employees using:

  • Search bar: Find employees by name or ID
  • Role tabs: Filter by Staff, Porters, or Forklift Operators
  • Employer filter: View employees by employer

Team Management

Teams are the foundation of efficient shift management in ShiftMaster . This section covers creating, managing, and optimizing teams.

Team Composition

A standard team consists of:

  • 1 Staff Member - Primary team coordinator
  • 2 Porters - Handle luggage and cargo movement
  • 1 Forklift Operator - Certified equipment operator
  • Optional Supervisor - Team leader when needed
Team Requirements

These requirements can be customized in Settings > Team Rules based on your organization's needs for different locations, areas, or shifts.

Creating a Team

  1. Navigate to the Shift Schedule section
  2. Select the date for the new team
  3. Click Add Team
  4. Select the shift type (Morning or Night)
  5. Choose the location and area
  6. Add team members:
    • Select 1 Staff Member
    • Select 2 Porters
    • Select 1 Forklift Operator
    • Optionally add a Supervisor
  7. Click Save Team
Team Creation Demo

Team Members

Viewing and Managing Teams

To view or edit existing teams:

  1. Navigate to the Teams section
  2. Use the date picker to select the desired date
  3. Apply filters if needed (Location, Area, Shift)
  4. Click on a team to view details
  5. Use the Edit button to make changes
  6. Click Delete to remove a team

Team Rules

Team Rules allow you to define different team compositions for various scenarios:

  • Different locations may require different team sizes
  • Certain areas might need additional staff
  • Night shifts could have different requirements than day shifts

To create a Team Rule:

  1. Navigate to Settings > Team Rules
  2. Click Add Rule
  3. Define the criteria (Date, Location, Area, Shift)
  4. Set the required number of each role
  5. Save the rule

Employee Assignments

The Assignments feature allows you to assign employees to specific shifts, locations, and areas before organizing them into teams.

Creating an Assignment

  1. Navigate to the Assign section
  2. Select the assignment date
  3. Click Add Assignment
  4. Fill in the assignment details:
    • Select the employee
    • Choose the shift (Morning or Night)
    • Select location and area
  5. Click Save Assignment
Create Assignment

Bulk Assignments

For assigning multiple employees at once:

  1. Navigate to the Assign section
  2. Select the assignment date
  3. Click Bulk Assign
  4. Select the shift, location, and area
  5. Choose multiple employees from the list
  6. Click Save Assignments
Assignment vs. Team

Assignments indicate when and where an employee is scheduled to work, while Teams organize assigned employees into functional groups. Employees must be assigned before they can be added to teams.

Viewing and Filtering Assignments

To view assignments:

  1. Navigate to the Assign section
  2. Select the date you want to view
  3. Use filters to narrow down by:
    • Location
    • Area
    • Shift type
  4. Click on an assignment to view details

Auto Generation

ShiftMaster's Auto Generation feature helps you quickly create complete schedules based on your predefined rules and employee availability.

How Auto Generation Works

The system uses intelligent algorithms to:

  • Distribute shifts fairly among employees
  • Match employee skills with shift requirements
  • Consider employee preferences and availability
  • Ensure all team composition rules are followed
  • Balance workload across locations and areas
Before You Generate

For best results, ensure you have:

  1. All employees properly entered in the system
  2. Team rules configured for your locations and areas
  3. Employee availability up to date
  4. Any blackout dates or special requirements noted

Running Auto Generation

  1. Navigate to the Automation section
  2. Select the date range for generation
  3. Choose locations and areas to include
  4. Select shift types (Morning, Night, or both)
  5. Click Generate Schedule
  6. Review the proposed schedule
  7. Click Apply Schedule to confirm
Auto Generation Demo
to

Reviewing Generated Schedules

After generation, carefully review the proposed schedule:

  • Check for any conflicts or unusual patterns
  • Verify that all required roles are filled
  • Ensure employee preferences were respected where possible
  • Make manual adjustments if needed before applying

Manual Adjustments

After auto-generation, you can still make manual changes:

  1. Navigate to the generated schedule
  2. Click on any shift to edit it
  3. Add, remove, or replace team members
  4. Save your changes

Best Practices

Following these best practices will help you get the most value from ShiftMaster and ensure smooth workforce management.

Scheduling Best Practices

Effective Scheduling
  • Plan ahead: Create schedules at least one week in advance
  • Balance workload: Distribute shifts fairly among employees
  • Consider preferences: Accommodate employee availability when possible
  • Review patterns: Regularly analyze shift assignments for optimization
  • Communicate changes: Notify employees promptly of any schedule updates

Team Composition Tips

  • Mix experienced and new team members for knowledge sharing
  • Ensure proper coverage for all required roles
  • Consider personality dynamics when forming teams
  • Rotate team members periodically to build versatility
  • Document any special team requirements in Team Rules

Employee Management

Keeping Employee Data Current

  • Regularly update contact information
  • Maintain accurate role and certification records
  • Track employee preferences and availability
  • Document performance notes for reference

Using Auto Generation Effectively

  1. Ensure all employee data is up to date before generating
  2. Set comprehensive Team Rules for different scenarios
  3. Review generated schedules carefully before applying
  4. Make manual adjustments as needed for special cases
  5. Save frequently used configurations as templates
Schedule Optimization

Use these techniques to improve your schedules:

Shift Rotation
Rotate employees between morning and night shifts
Skill Balancing
Ensure each team has the right mix of skills
Cross-Training
Schedule employees in different roles occasionally

Reporting Insights

Regularly review these key reports to optimize your workforce management:

  • Overtime Analysis: Monitor and control overtime costs
  • Employee Hours: Ensure fair distribution of work hours
  • Shift Coverage: Identify gaps in your scheduling
  • Team Performance: Assess productivity by team
